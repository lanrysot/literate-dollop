Minimalism

Check out my work using Minimalism — it is often used by companies like Apple and Google for clean, premium, distraction-free designs. It uses extremely low color contrast, simple layouts, and focuses attention on content, function, and clarity.

Cinematic Style

Check out my work using Cinematic style — it is often used by companies like Tesla, Apple, and luxury tech brands for cinematic experiences. It combines a dark base color, deep ash tones, and a soft dull highlight of the same color to create a refined futuristic atmosphere.

03

Flat 2D Style

Check out my work using Flat 2D Style — it is often used by companies like Google and Microsoft for simple communication-focused visuals. It uses flat shapes, minimal depth, and simple illustrations to avoid distraction and make information instantly understandable.

04

Monochromatic

Check out my work using Monochromatic Design — it is often used by companies like Sony and premium fashion brands for elegant visual identity. It focuses on one main color using different shades, tones, or inverted variations to create harmony and strong visual consistency.

Futuristic

Check out my work using Futuristic Design — it is often used by companies like NVIDIA, SpaceX, and AI technology brands to represent advanced innovation. It uses digital interfaces, glowing elements, sci-fi typography, and supercomputer-inspired visuals to create a feeling of being in a future technological world.

06

Neo-Brutalism

Check out my work using Neo-Brutalism — it is often used by companies like Figma, Dropbox, and modern creative startups for bold digital experiences. It uses raw layouts, strong typography, high contrast colors, thick borders, oversized elements, and intentionally imperfect designs to create a rebellious and attention-grabbing visual style.

07

3D Style

Check out my work using 3D Style — it is often used by companies like Apple, Meta, and Adobe for immersive product experiences and modern brand visuals. It uses depth, lighting, realistic materials, and dimensional objects to create a tactile feeling where digital elements appear physically present and interactive.
